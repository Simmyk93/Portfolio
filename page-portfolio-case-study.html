Did You Get It?

A case study inspired by users post-pandemic travel goals.

UX/UI Researchers & Designers: Rowan Miller & Simran Harwell

Problem: Users need a way to consolidate important travel documents, to-do lists, and packing items without using multiple services.

Solution: A collaborative travel app designed for users to make customizable lists, upload documents, and learn about the weather and safety conditions of your location before you travel.

Impact: Make travel planning less stressful by having your itinerary, packing lists, travel information and weather predictions all in one place. Share your travel plans with friends and family who are travelling with you or create lists for them to ensure they don't forget anything!

Discovery

"Did You Get It?" was designed to achieve a thorough packing experience for all travelers. We have observed that travelers get overwhelmed with remembering every moving part of preparing for a trip, which can disrupt/obstruct the trip and even compromise a traveler's mobility or safety. How might we help Did You Get It?Users account for their travel necessities without the stress of forgetting something.

Define

Interviewed seven users ranging from 20 years old to 50 years old, helped define common user pain points and gave direction to our design.

User research helped us refine our proto-persona to create a new user-persona.

Ideation

Our user, Clara, needs a way to account for all of her travel necessities, because forgetting a necessity while on a trip can ruin the experience and possibly compromise her safety or ability to travel.

How might we help Did You Get It? users account for their travel necessities so they have a good, safe trip?.

Storyboard

Clara and her husband Tim are planning a trip to Nicaragua. They’re about to head to the airport, but Tim has to do some last-minute packing before they leave.

Testing & Iterating

We designed lo-fi sketches to get more user feedback.

By doing additional testing with lo-fi and mid-fi wireframes, our users were able to provide us with key:

  • Users liked our app idea and felt like their needs were being addressed.
  • Navigation was intuitive.
  • Liked the visual cues over text (i.e. icons, arrows)
  • Found some glitches while in the prototype including some buttons that were not clickable.

Final Prototype & Closing Thoughts

The concept behind Did You Get It? Has value with most users, based on qualitative data from interviews and user tests.

Our app features were very broad -- we couldn’t fit every idea into the final prototype

Re-testing the prototype was essential for finding minor errors in lo and mid-fi iterations
